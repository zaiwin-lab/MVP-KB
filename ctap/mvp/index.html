In Plain Terms

So, what exactly is CTAP?

CTAP stands for Corporate TVET Advancement Program — a fully-managed service that turns your existing workers into government-certified, nationally-recognised tradespeople, without pulling your HR team into the paperwork. Here's what each word means for your business:

C

Corporate

Built for employers, not walk-in students. We work directly with your HR team — one point of contact, one process, zero red tape for your staff.

T

TVET

Technical & Vocational Education and Training — Malaysia's official skills certification system. Your workers earn SKM / DKM credentials issued by JPK and recognised nationwide.

A

Advancement

A structured ladder — SKM Level 2 → Level 3 → DKM Diploma — that grows your workers' skills, pay grade and loyalty, year after year.

P

Program

A complete, end-to-end managed service: assessment, HRD Corp funding application, training, certification and graduation — you sign two documents, we do the rest.

Simple Process

How CTAP Works

From your first call to your workers' certificate ceremony — we handle everything.

1

Free Workforce Assessment

We visit your site, assess your workers' current skill levels, map them to JPK's National Occupational Skills Standards (NOSS), and calculate how many can enrol using your HRD Corp levy. No cost, no commitment.

2

MOU Signing + HRD Corp Application

We prepare the Employer MOU, Training Bond templates, and submit the SBL-Khas grant application on your behalf. HRD Corp typically approves within 48–72 hours. You sign two documents. We do the rest.

3

Blended Training Begins

Our trainers come to your site 2 days/week for hands-on practical sessions. Theory is delivered via WhatsApp micro-learning — no need for workers to leave site for long periods. Training is built around your shift patterns.

4

JPK Assessment + Certificate Issuance

At Month 5–6, JPK-accredited assessors conduct formal competency assessments (written + practical). Results submitted to JPK. SKM certificates issued. Workers are now nationally recognised — and far less likely to leave.

5

Certificate Ceremony + Level 3 Enrolment

We organise a branded certificate award ceremony at your premises — a genuine milestone that builds loyalty. Workers immediately begin planning their Level 3 pathway. The career ladder is set, and they're climbing it with you.

FAQ

Questions Employers Always Ask

Is CTAP really free? What's the actual catch? +
There is no catch. You pay only the 4% SBL-Khas service charge — for example, RM1,680 out of pocket for 10 employees enrolled at RM4,200 each. The remaining RM42,000 is claimed directly from your existing HRD Corp levy balance. You are already paying this levy every month. CTAP helps you use it meaningfully before the 15% year-end deduction kicks in.
What if my workers complete the program and then resign? +
We provide Training Bond Agreement templates (lawyer-reviewed, compliant with Malaysia Contracts Act 1950) as part of your onboarding package. Workers who leave within the bond period (typically 18–36 months) repay a pro-rated portion of the training cost. Courts in Malaysia have upheld these agreements provided they are voluntary, reasonable, and non-restrictive of future employment. The bond also significantly reduces resignation intention — workers with clear career ladders are 40% less likely to leave.
How is CTAP different from sending my staff to GIATMARA or Politeknik? +
Public institutions like GIATMARA, ILP, and Politeknik primarily serve full-time students — fresh school leavers. They are not equipped to train your 35-year-old experienced welder who works 8am–5pm and can't take 6 months off. CTAP trains your existing workforce at your workplace, around your operating schedule. We also manage all HRD Corp paperwork, JPK assessment coordination, and Training Bond documentation on your behalf. Public institutions don't offer this B2B service.
My company has only 25 employees. Do I qualify? +
Yes. Companies with 10–49 employees can voluntarily register with HRD Corp at a 0.5% levy rate, or you may already be registered. Even without a large levy balance, we can design a smaller cohort (5 workers) priced at RM6,000/employee for pilot batches. We also assist voluntary registration applications. Contact us via WhatsApp and we'll assess your HRD Corp status for free.
Which industries and skills areas does CTAP cover in Sarawak? +
Our current Year 1 focus for Sarawak is: Construction (civil, electrical, plumbing), Hospitality (F&B service, front office, housekeeping), and Manufacturing (process plant operations). We are adding Oil & Gas (mechanical/instrumentation), Healthcare (nursing aid), and Agriculture (agrotechnology) in Phase 2. All programs are mapped to JPK's NOSS — the official National Occupational Skills Standards used for SKM/DKM certification.
How long does the HRD Corp approval take? +
Under the SBL-Khas scheme, HRD Corp approves applications within 48–72 hours if all documentation is complete. CTAP prepares and submits the application on your behalf, so from the day you sign the MOU, training can begin within 2 weeks. The only requirement is that the application is submitted at least 1 working day before training starts.
Does CTAP disrupt my operations? +
No. Training is scheduled around your operational requirements. Practical sessions run 2 days per week (half-day blocks), typically during quieter periods. Theory is delivered via WhatsApp messages workers can read at their own pace. We work with your HR team to schedule around peak demand, shift rotations, and project deadlines. Most employers report zero operational disruption within 3 weeks of program start.
