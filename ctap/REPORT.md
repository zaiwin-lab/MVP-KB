## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The Opportunity:** Malaysia spends RM2.62 billion annually via HRD Corp on workforce training (2025), yet employer-sponsored TVET qualification pathways remain almost non-existent as a structured B2B product. Sarawak has a 20% skilled workforce against a 30% target by 2030 — that is a gap of 210,000+ workers who need upskilling.

**The Product:** CTAP (Corporate TVET Advancement Program) is a turnkey B2B service that enables Malaysian employers to enrol their workers into structured SKM Level 2 → Level 3 → DKM Diploma qualification pathways — fully funded through HRD Corp levy, delivered on-site and online, assessed by JPK-accredited assessors, resulting in nationally recognised Malaysian Skills Certificates.

**The Business Model:** KOBIS Berhad operates as HRD Corp–registered training provider + JPK-accredited assessment centre. Employers pay zero out-of-pocket because CTAP fees are structured to be 100% claimable via their HRD Corp levy accounts under the SBL-Khas scheme.

**The Unfair Advantage:** Structuring program costs to match the employer's levy balance transforms CTAP from a cost-centre to a free employee benefit — removing the #1 objection in B2B training sales.

## 3. HR PAIN POINTS — EMPLOYER PROBLEM STATEMENT

### Pain Point 1: Attrition Is Costing Employers Millions

- Malaysia voluntary attrition rate: **16.2% in 2023** (Aon, 900+ companies)
- WTW data: 18.5% voluntary turnover in 2023 in Malaysia
- Employee replacement cost (SHRM): **6–9 months of salary**
- For a RM3,500/month worker: replacement cost = **RM21,000–31,500 per person**
- Company with 200 workers, 16% annual turnover = 32 leavers/year
- Annual attrition cost: **32 × RM26,000 = RM832,000/year** — before productivity loss

**CTAP ROI argument:** If CTAP costs RM4,500/employee/year and reduces attrition from 16% to 10% among program participants (based on Singapore SkillsFuture data showing 64% linked training to career advancement):
- Attrition reduction: 32 → 20 leavers/year = 12 fewer leavers
- Savings: 12 × RM26,000 = **RM312,000 saved**
- CTAP investment (20 enrolled × RM4,500): **RM90,000**
- **Net ROI = +RM222,000 / 3.5x return in Year 1 alone**

### Pain Point 2: "Train Them, Then They Leave" Fear

This is the #1 objection to corporate training investment in Malaysia.

**CTAP's solution:** Training Bond Agreement
- Legally enforceable under Malaysia Contracts Act 1950
- Requirements: voluntary consent, reasonable terms, non-restraint of trade
- Structure: 2–3 year bond, pro-rated repayment if employee leaves early
- Example: Employee completes SKM Level 2 (RM3,500 investment). If they leave within 2 years, they repay RM1,750–3,500 depending on tenure
- KOBIS provides standardised bond template as part of employer onboarding package

### Pain Point 3: Compliance Pressure

- **Construction:** CIDB Green Card mandatory; workers without it cannot enter sites
- **Healthcare:** MOH-regulated qualification requirements
- **O&G:** PETROS Local Content Policy requires documented upskilling of Sarawakians
- **Manufacturing:** ISO/IEC standards increasingly require certified operators

Compliance is a forcing function. CTAP converts mandatory compliance into a qualification pathway.

### Pain Point 4: Younger Workforce Expects Career Progression

- Malaysia Gen Z and Millennial workers cite "no career advancement" as top reason for leaving
- CTAP offers a visible 3-step progression: SKM L2 → SKM L3 → DKM Diploma
- Milestone-based promotion criteria linked to qualification — employers can tie SKM Level 3 to a pay increment or supervisor role
- This transforms CTAP from "training" to a structured career ladder

### Pain Point 5: Skills Mismatch

- World Bank (2024) confirmed Malaysia has systemic mismatch between education outputs and industry needs
- Sarawak-specific: workers trained for manufacturing but working in construction, misaligned competencies
- CTAP is built around NOSS (National Occupational Skills Standards) — industry-defined standards approved by JPK
- Every SKM/DKM qualification maps directly to an occupational competency

## 4. HRD CORP FUNDING FRAMEWORK

### 4.1 HRD Corp Levy — How It Works

| Employer Type | Levy Rate |
|--------------|-----------|
| Mandatory (50+ employees in covered sectors) | 1% of monthly wages |
| Voluntary (10–49 employees) | 0.5% of monthly wages |
| Covered sectors | Manufacturing, Services, Mining, Construction, Finance, Transport, etc. |

**Calculation example:**
- 100 employees × RM3,500 avg wage × 12 months × 1% = **RM42,000/year levy balance**
- Employer must use this or lose 15% of unused levy (new rule from March 2025)
- This creates urgency: employers are incentivised to spend their levy balance

### 4.2 SBL-Khas Scheme — The CTAP Funding Vehicle

**How it works:**
1. CTAP (as registered HRD Corp training provider) registers program on e-TRIS portal
2. Employer applies for SBL-Khas grant — approval in 48–72 hours
3. HRD Corp pays CTAP directly from employer's levy account (4% service charge applies)
4. Employer net cost: **ZERO** (training fully covered by levy they already paid)

**Key rules:**
- Application must be submitted at least 1 day before training starts
- Training provider must be HRD Corp–registered
- Program must be an "HRD Corp Claimable Course"
- Costs are governed by an Allowable Cost Matrix

### 4.3 CTAP Provider Registration Requirements

| Requirement | Cost / Detail |
|-------------|---------------|
| SSM registration (Sdn Bhd) | Already done (KOBIS Berhad) |
| HRD Corp provider registration | RM1,000/year (main office) |
| Business nature must include "training" | Amendment to M&A if needed |
| Trainer TTT certification | RM2,500–4,500 per trainer (5-day course) |
| Program accreditation via JPK | Required for SKM/DKM qualification delivery |
| JPK accreditation application | RM5,000–20,000 (one-time, per program area) |
| NOSS compliance | Program content must align with approved NOSS |

**Timeline to become operational:**
- SSM/HRD Corp registration: 2–4 weeks
- JPK accreditation: 3–6 months (this is the critical path)
- **MVP strategy:** Partner with an existing JPK-accredited centre for Year 1 while KOBIS pursues own accreditation

### 4.4 SBL-Khas for TVET Certification Programs

TVET qualification programs (SKM/DKM) are explicitly claimable under SBL-Khas provided the training provider is JPK-accredited AND HRD Corp–registered.

**Dual accreditation is the moat:** Few private providers have both JPK + HRD Corp registration. This is CTAP's competitive barrier once obtained.

## 6. RETENTION BENEFITS & ROI FOR EMPLOYERS

### 6.1 ROI Calculator Template

**Inputs:**
```
A = Number of employees enrolled in CTAP
B = Average monthly salary of enrolled employees (MYR)
C = Current annual attrition rate (%)
D = Expected attrition rate post-CTAP (%) — use 60% of current as conservative estimate
E = CTAP annual cost per employee (MYR)
```

**Calculations:**
```
Current annual leavers (from enrolled pool) = A × C
Post-CTAP annual leavers = A × D
Leavers prevented = A × (C - D)
Replacement cost per employee = B × 7.5 (7.5 months salary — SHRM midpoint)
Annual savings = Leavers prevented × Replacement cost
CTAP investment = A × E
Net ROI = Annual savings - CTAP investment
ROI % = (Annual savings / CTAP investment) × 100
```

**Worked Example (Kuching Construction Company, 200 workers, avg RM3,500):**

```
A = 25 enrolled
B = RM3,500/month
C = 18% attrition (industry high)
D = 11% (post-CTAP estimate — 40% reduction)
E = RM4,200/year

Leavers prevented = 25 × (18% - 11%) = 1.75 → round to 2 per year
Replacement cost = RM3,500 × 7.5 = RM26,250 per person
Annual savings = 2 × RM26,250 = RM52,500
CTAP investment = 25 × RM4,200 = RM105,000
(But HRD Corp funded = employer pays ~RM4,200 net)
Net ROI vs actual employer cost = RM52,500 / RM4,200 = 12.5x
```

### 6.4 Training Bond — Legal Framework

Training bonds are **legal and enforceable** in Malaysia under Contracts Act 1950, provided:
1. Employee signs voluntarily (no coercion)
2. Bond period is reasonable (2–3 years for a 6–12 month program is acceptable)
3. Repayment clause is proportional (not punitive)
4. Does not restrain trade post-employment (employee can work elsewhere, just repays cost)

**Recommended CTAP bond structure:**
- SKM Level 2 bond: 18-month service commitment, pro-rated repayment
- SKM Level 3 bond: additional 18-month commitment
- DKM bond: 24-month commitment from graduation
- Total: employee completing full pathway has soft commitment of ~5 years from program start
- KOBIS provides standard bond template as part of employer onboarding documents

## 8. IMPLEMENTATION FRAMEWORK

### 8.1 Program Architecture

```
CTAP Corporate TVET Advancement Program
│
├── Tier 1: SKM Level 2 (Foundation)
│   ├── Duration: 6 months (200 hours)
│   ├── Delivery: 3 days/week on-site at employer premises OR
│   │             Blended (online theory + on-site practical)
│   ├── Assessment: JPK-accredited assessors (written + practical)
│   └── Output: Sijil Kemahiran Malaysia Level 2 (national recognition)
│
├── Tier 2: SKM Level 3 (Intermediate)
│   ├── Duration: 6 months (240 hours)
│   ├── Pre-requisite: SKM Level 2 certificate
│   ├── Delivery: Same blended model
│   ├── Assessment: JPK assessors
│   └── Output: Sijil Kemahiran Malaysia Level 3
```

### 8.3 Delivery Model

**Phase A — Assessment (Week 1–2):**
- Skill gap analysis via standardised CTAP Assessment Tool
- Mapping existing worker competencies to NOSS standards
- Recognition of Prior Achievement (RPA/PPT) — workers with partial competencies can skip to higher level
- Output: Individual Learning Plan per employee

**Phase B — Training Delivery (Month 1–6):**
- Theory modules: online via LMS (WhatsApp-friendly micro-learning for non-desk workers)
- Practical sessions: 2 days/week at employer site OR CTAP assessment centre
- Progress tracking: monthly competency check-ins
- Trainer visits employer site every 2 weeks for practical assessments

**Phase C — Assessment & Certification (Month 5–6):**
- JPK-accredited assessors conduct formal competency assessment
- Written test + practical demonstration
- Results submitted to JPK → SKM certificate issued (6–8 weeks processing)

**Phase D — Progression Planning:**
- Certificate award ceremony (employer-branded event — creates social media moment)
- Level 3 enrollment begins immediately
- Training bond renewal signed
