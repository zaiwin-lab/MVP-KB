## 2. What the Business Does

CTAP is a B2B workforce qualification service. KOBIS Berhad sells to HR departments and business owners in Malaysia, enabling them to enrol their existing employees into government-accredited TVET qualification pathways — SKM Level 2, SKM Level 3, and DKM Diploma — delivered at the employer's workplace or through blended online/on-site learning.

The core value proposition is that the entire program cost is structured to be claimable through the employer's HRD Corp levy balance under the SBL-Khas scheme. A 100-employee company paying RM42,000/year in HRD Corp levy can enrol 10 workers into CTAP for RM42,000, paid entirely from their levy — their out-of-pocket cost is under RM2,000.

Delivery model: CTAP trainers visit employer sites 2 days/week for practical training. Theory is delivered via WhatsApp-based micro-learning and PDF workbooks. Assessment is conducted by JPK-accredited assessors. Certificates are issued by JPK (Jabatan Pembangunan Kemahiran / Department of Skills Development), Malaysia's national skills authority.

## 3. Target Market

**Primary Customer:**
- Role: HR Manager, Training Manager, or Business Owner/CEO
- Company size: 50–300 employees
- Location: Kuching, Sarawak (Phase 1) → Sarawak statewide (Phase 2) → Sabah + Peninsula (Phase 3)
- Industry: Construction, Oil & Gas, Manufacturing, Hospitality, Healthcare
- Key pain: High staff attrition (avg 16–18%/year in Malaysia), no structured career path for workers, HRD Corp levy sitting idle, CIDB/compliance pressure
- Desired outcome: Retain skilled workers longer, meet compliance requirements, use HRD Corp levy meaningfully

**Secondary Customer:**
- Workers aged 25–40 in semi-skilled roles who want a formal qualification but can't attend full-time TVET
- Benefit: National recognition (SKM/DKM), career advancement, higher pay bracket

**Market Size:**
- Sarawak: ~2,000–3,000 eligible employers; 210,000+ workers needing upskilling to meet PCDS 2030
- Malaysia: ~500,000 HRD Corp-registered employers; 6–8 million semi-skilled workers without formal qualifications
- Serviceable Obtainable Market (Year 1–3): RM652K → RM3.5M → RM12M revenue

## 10. Key Risks

1. **JPK accreditation delay** (HIGH) → Mitigation: Sub-contract to existing JPK-accredited centre for Year 1; parallel-track own accreditation application
2. **Employer HRD Corp levy insufficient** → Mitigation: Pre-screen employers for levy balance before MOU; offer partial funding model for levy-lite companies
3. **Employee training dropout** → Mitigation: Training bond + employer-linked incentive (salary increment tied to certification milestone)
4. **Long B2B sales cycle** → Mitigation: Lead with compliance angle (CIDB) which is non-negotiable and accelerates decisions; target CEOs of SMEs, not procurement committees
5. **Competitor enters market** → Mitigation: Lock in exclusive referral partnerships with CIDB, STA, SCCCI Sarawak; build employer base fast in Year 1
