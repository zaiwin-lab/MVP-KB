The Problem We Solve

From Policy to Practice: The Gap

Despite Sarawak’s remarkable policy momentum, critical gaps persist between ambitious frameworks and ground-level implementation.

  • Limited ESG capacity among SMEs and local businesses
  • Difficulty translating green projects into bankable propositions
  • Greenwashing risks in under-regulated reporting environments
  • Insufficient cross-sector dialogue connecting policy with practice
  • Knowledge and skills gaps in green economy workforce
🎯
The Forum’s Role

Borneo ESG Forum 2026 bridges this gap — convening the right stakeholders, facilitating substantive dialogue, producing actionable outcomes.
