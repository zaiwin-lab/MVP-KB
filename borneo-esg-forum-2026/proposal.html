Section 01

Executive Summary

The Borneo ESG Forum 2026 is a landmark two-day regional conference dedicated to scaling Borneo’s green economy and advancing Environmental, Social & Governance (ESG) leadership across the region. Scheduled for 14–15 October 2026 in Kuching, Sarawak, Malaysia, the Forum is a paid, curated event designed to convene 200+ serious sustainability practitioners across government, finance, business, and civil society.

The Forum arrives at a pivotal moment: Sarawak has just launched its Sarawak Sustainability Blueprint 2030 and Sarawak Energy Transition Policy (SET-P), positioning the state as Southeast Asia’s most ambitious sub-national sustainability actor. The Borneo ESG Forum 2026 amplifies and operationalises this ambition — providing the critical dialogue platform that converts policy into action.

Organised by ZAYA Consultation Group and CIC Capital Consultancy, with sustainability partners Centre for Sustainability Research and ESG ADR Forum, the Borneo ESG Forum 2026 is structured around seven interdisciplinary focus areas: Green Economy & Investment, Renewable Energy Transition, Nature & Carbon Solutions, ESG Governance & Policy, SMEs & Community Development, Innovation Technology & Data, and ESG Accountability & Greenwashing.

Through two days of keynotes, panel discussions, fireside chats, and case studies, the Forum will produce actionable insights, forge lasting partnerships, and contribute meaningfully to Sarawak’s net-zero 2050 ambition.

Section 02

Background & Context: Borneo’s ESG Moment

2.1 The Global ESG Imperative

ESG has moved from a niche investment framework to a fundamental operating requirement for governments, corporations, and communities worldwide. The UNPRI now counts over 5,000 signatories managing USD 120 trillion in assets. Green bond issuance surpassed USD 500 billion annually. The Paris Agreement’s net-zero targets have become binding commitments driving regulatory, financial, and social transformation at every level. Southeast Asia — home to critical biodiversity, significant carbon stocks, and rapidly growing economies — sits at the intersection of global opportunity and obligation.

2.2 Sarawak’s Green Economy Leadership

Sarawak has emerged as Malaysia’s most ambitious sustainability actor, building a comprehensive policy ecosystem that positions the state as Southeast Asia’s premier green economy destination. Key milestones in Sarawak’s sustainability journey include:

  • Sarawak Sustainability Blueprint 2030 (May 2025) — Malaysia’s first state-level dedicated sustainability masterplan, comprising 10 strategic thrusts, 48 strategies, and 111 actionable plans spanning energy, agriculture, circular economy, and social equity.
  • Sarawak Energy Transition Policy (SET-P) (August 2025) — A landmark policy establishing a 60% renewable energy capacity mix target by 2030, aligning energy policy with climate commitments and investment attraction.
  • Sarawak Hydrogen Economy Roadmap — Positioning Sarawak as a regional hydrogen production and export hub, leveraging abundant hydropower and strategic geographic location.
  • Post-COVID Development Strategy 2030 (PCDS 2030) — The overarching state economic blueprint integrating ESG principles across all sectors of development.
  • Environment (Reduction of GHG Emissions) Bill — First in Malaysia, establishing a legal framework for carbon reporting and future carbon tax mechanisms.

“We do not see sustainability and economic growth as opposing forces. In Sarawak, they are two sides of the same coin — and we are building the policy ecosystem to prove it.”

YB Datuk Dr Hazland bin Abang Hipni · Deputy Minister, MEESty Sarawak

2.4 The Gap: From Policy to Practice

Despite Sarawak’s remarkable policy momentum, a critical gap persists between ambitious policy frameworks and ground-level implementation. Challenges include: limited ESG capacity among SMEs and local businesses; difficulty translating green projects into bankable investment propositions; greenwashing risks in an under-regulated reporting environment; and insufficient cross-sector dialogue connecting policymakers with investors and practitioners. The Borneo ESG Forum 2026 is specifically designed to bridge this gap.

Section 05

Strategic Alignment

The Borneo ESG Forum 2026 is not simply another sustainability conference. It is strategically aligned with — and directly supportive of — Sarawak’s most ambitious policy frameworks and development goals.

📑
Sarawak Sustainability Blueprint 2030
The Forum’s 7 focus areas map directly onto the Blueprint’s 10 strategic thrusts. The Forum creates the stakeholder dialogue platform the Blueprint requires to translate its 111 actionable plans into real-world implementation. Sessions on green investment, renewable energy, nature-based solutions, and ESG governance directly address Blueprint priorities.
⚡
Sarawak Energy Transition Policy (SET-P)
Sessions 3 and 6 directly address the investment facilitation and workforce readiness challenges central to SET-P implementation. The Fireside Chat on “Financing Borneo’s Green Economy” is designed to catalyse the private capital flows that SET-P’s clean energy targets require. The Forum brings together the banks, DFIs, and developers SET-P needs to succeed.
🌎
PCDS 2030 — Post-COVID Development Strategy
The Forum’s focus on SME empowerment (Sessions 5 & 7) and community-inclusive growth directly supports PCDS 2030’s inclusive development mandate. The Forum creates pathways for SMEs to access green economy opportunities, aligning with PCDS targets for employment creation and household income growth.
🍏
MEESty Mission: Clean Energy & Sustainable Environment
MEESty’s role in policy formulation for energy and environmental sustainability is reinforced by the Forum’s convening of the exact stakeholders MEESty needs engaged: investors, corporates, NGOs, and academia. The Forum extends MEESty’s own convening work (International Energy Week, H2EF) into the broader ESG governance and green economy space.
🍀
Net Zero 2050 & Carbon Market Development
Session 4’s focus on nature-based and carbon projects directly advances Sarawak’s carbon market ambitions and GHG inventory goals. Session 8 on greenwashing builds the accountability infrastructure needed for credible carbon markets. The Forum is a catalyst for Sarawak’s carbon economy.
